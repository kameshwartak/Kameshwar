Required Documents for Shram Suikriti in Nepal

Shram Suikriti (Labour Approval) is a compulsory authorization issued by the Government of Nepal for citizens who are travelling abroad for foreign employment. It confirms that the worker’s job offer, employer, salary, contract, and visa are verified and registered under Nepal’s Foreign Employment Act. The Department of Foreign Employment (DoFE) manages this system to ensure that Nepali workers migrate through a legal and secure process.

Before applying for labour approval, applicants must prepare several important documents. These documents are required to verify the authenticity of the employment opportunity, ensure worker safety, and confirm compliance with Nepal’s foreign employment regulations. Submitting correct documents helps avoid delays or rejection of the labour approval application.

Why Documents Are Required for Shram Suikriti

The Government of Nepal requires specific documents to protect migrant workers from illegal recruitment, fraud, and unsafe working conditions abroad. These documents help verify the worker’s identity, employment details, insurance coverage, and health condition before departure.

Proper documentation also ensures that workers are registered in the government system, which allows them to receive support from the Foreign Employment Welfare Fund in case of accidents, emergencies, or disputes while working overseas.

Complete List of Required Documents for Shram Suikriti

Applicants must prepare the following documents before applying for labour approval:

1. Valid Nepali Passport
A passport is the primary identity document required for foreign employment. The passport must be valid for at least six months from the date of travel and should contain accurate personal details.

2. Valid Work Visa
A work visa issued by the destination country is required to confirm that the worker is legally allowed to work in that country. Tourist visas are not accepted for labour approval.

3. Employment Contract (Job Agreement)
The employment contract signed between the worker and the employer must include important details such as salary, job position, working hours, accommodation, insurance, and contract duration.

4. Insurance Policy for Foreign Employment
Workers must purchase a mandatory insurance policy that covers accidental death, disability, and medical emergencies during the foreign employment period.

5. Medical Report from Government-Approved Medical Center
A medical fitness certificate from an authorized medical institution approved by the Government of Nepal is required. This report confirms that the worker is physically and mentally fit for overseas employment.

6. Pre-Departure Orientation Training Certificate
Workers must complete Pre-Departure Orientation Training (PDOT) which provides information about labour laws, workplace safety, culture, and rights in the destination country.

7. Citizenship Certificate
A copy of the Nepali citizenship certificate is required to verify the applicant’s identity and nationality during the application process.

8. Passport Size Photographs
Recent passport-size photographs may be required when submitting documents or completing the labour approval process.

Additional Documents for Certain Countries

Some destination countries may require additional documents depending on their immigration and labour regulations. These may include embassy verification, demand letters from the employer, or recruitment agency approval documents.

Applicants should always check with their recruitment agency or the Department of Foreign Employment to confirm if any additional documents are required for their destination country.

Where to Apply for Labour Approval

Workers can apply for labour approval through the official Foreign Employment Information Management System (FEIMS) portal provided by the Government of Nepal.

https://feims.dofe.gov.np

Through this online system, applicants can register their profile, upload documents, submit labour approval applications, and track their application status digitally.

Important Tips Before Submitting Documents

Before applying for Shram Suikriti, workers should carefully review all documents to ensure that names, passport numbers, and employment details match exactly across all documents. Any mismatch or incorrect information may delay the approval process.

It is also recommended to keep both digital and printed copies of all documents for future reference. Having complete and accurate documents will help workers obtain labour approval faster and avoid complications before departure.
