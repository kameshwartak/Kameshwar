Online Saram Suikiriti: Process, Required Documents and Official Fees

1. Shram Suikriti Process

Shram Suikriti (Labour Approval) is compulsory for all Nepali citizens going abroad for foreign employment. It is issued by the Department of Foreign Employment (DoFE) under the Ministry of Labour, Employment and Social Security. The process includes: securing a valid job offer and work visa, verification of documents (including embassy attestation where required), completing a government-approved medical examination, attending Pre-Departure Orientation Training (PDOT), purchasing mandatory insurance, and submitting the application through the official FEIMS portal (feims.dofe.gov.np) or via a licensed manpower agency. After verification, labour approval is granted electronically.

2. Required Documents for Shram Suikriti

Applicants must generally submit: a valid passport (minimum 6 months validity), valid work visa, employment contract, demand letter (for agency processing), medical fitness certificate from an approved medical center, Pre-Departure Orientation Training certificate, insurance policy (as per government coverage rules), and passport-size photographs. Additional documents may be required depending on the destination country and job category.

3. Official Government Fees (Latest)

As per current government provisions, the official Shram Suikriti fee is NPR 1,000 for new (individual) labour approval and NPR 500 for renewal. Besides the government fee, applicants must bear separate fixed charges for medical examination, insurance coverage, and orientation training as approved by the Government of Nepal. Workers are strongly advised to apply only through licensed manpower agencies or the official DoFE system to avoid fraud and overcharging.

Online Saram Suikiriti Renewal: Process, Required Documents and Official Fees

1. Shram Suikriti Renewal Process

Shram Suikriti Renewal is required for Nepali workers who are returning abroad for employment after completing their leave period in Nepal. The renewal is processed through the Department of Foreign Employment (DoFE) under the Ministry of Labour, Employment and Social Security. Applicants must have a valid re-entry visa or renewed employment contract from the same employer (or new contract if changed), updated insurance coverage, and valid medical fitness (if required). The application can be submitted online through the official FEIMS portal (feims.dofe.gov.np) or through licensed manpower agencies. Once documents are verified, the renewed labour approval is issued electronically.

2. Required Documents for Renewal

Required documents generally include: original passport (minimum 6 months validity), previous Shram Suikriti copy, valid re-entry visa or new work visa, renewed or updated employment contract, insurance policy as per government rule, and passport-size photographs. In some cases, updated medical reports may be required depending on the country and duration of stay.

3. Official Government Fees (Latest)

As per current government provisions, the official fee for Shram Suikriti Renewal is NPR 500. Additional costs may apply for insurance renewal, medical examination (if required), and service charges if processed through a licensed manpower agency. Workers are advised to use only the official DoFE system or authorized agencies to ensure safe and legal processing.

Online Passport Renewal: Process, Required Documents and Official Fees

1. Nepal E-Passport Application Process

Applying for a Nepali e-passport within Nepal is managed by the Department of Passports. Applicants must first complete the online pre-enrollment form, select the correct application category (New, Renewal, Lost/Stolen, or Damaged), choose 34 or 66 pages, and book an appointment date. After carefully filling in personal details as per Citizenship and National ID, applicants must upload scanned documents (under 300 KB). Once submitted, download and print the barcode/QR code form and visit the enrollment center on the scheduled date for biometric capture (photo, fingerprint, and signature). Corrections are allowed only before final approval; mistakes after approval require a new application and fee.

2. Required Documents for Passport (Within Nepal)

Applicants must present: printed application form with barcode/QR code (or original PDF), original Nepali Citizenship Certificate or Minor ID Card, original old passport (if previously issued), National Identity Card (NID) or NID number, and revenue payment receipt. For minors below 16 years, additional documents include district verification of Minor ID, citizenship of parents/guardian, and other supporting documents if required. All documents must match the submitted online details to avoid rejection or delay.

3. Official Government Passport Fees (Latest)

The official passport fee depends on age, type, and condition of the passport. For new or renewal applications, the fee is NPR 12,000 for 34 pages and NPR 20,000 for 66 pages. For minors under 10 years, the fee is NPR 9,500 (34 pages) and NPR 14,500 (66 pages). For lost or damaged passports, adults must pay NPR 17,000 (34 pages) or NPR 25,000 (66 pages), while minors under 10 must pay NPR 14,500 (34 pages) or NPR 19,500 (66 pages). If the passport error is caused by the issuing office, re-issuance is free of cost. Applicants are advised to verify official fees before payment.
